### ID 851 · LONG · 5M · hard

200 logs are stacked in the following manner: 20 logs in the bottom row, 19 in the next row, 18 in the row next to it and so on (see below figures). In how many rows are the 200 logs placed and how many logs are in the top row?

**Answer:** This forms an AP: 20, 19, 18, ... with $a=20$ and $d=-1$. We are given $S_n = 200$.
$S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a + (n-1)d] = 200 \Rightarrow \frac{n}{2}[40 + (n-1)(-1)] = 200 \Rightarrow n(41 - n) = 400$.
$41n - n^2 = 400 \Rightarrow n^2 - 41n + 400 = 0$.
Factorize: $(n-16)(n-25) = 0$. So $n=16$ or $n=25$.
Check the number of logs in the top row for each case: $a_n = a + (n-1)d$.
If $n=25$, $a_{25} = 20 + 24(-1) = 20 - 24 = -4$ (not possible to have negative logs).
If $n=16$, $a_{16} = 20 + 15(-1) = 20 - 15 = 5$ (valid).
So, there are 16 rows and 5 logs in the top row.

### ID 1364 · LONG · 5M · hard

A triangle ABC is drawn to circumscribe a circle of radius 4 cm such that the segments BD and DC are of lengths 10 cm and 8 cm respectively. Find the lengths of the sides AB and AC, if it is given that area ∆ABC = 90 cm².

![diagram](http://localhost:8000/media/question_images/p60_q1_a_triangle_abc_is_drawn_to_cir.png)

**Answer:** Let's denote the points where the incircle meets AB and AC as E and F respectively. Then, AE = AF = x (tangents from the same external point are equal). Similarly, BE = BD = 10 cm and CD = CF = 8 cm. We have, AB = x + 10, AC = x + 8. Now, area of ∆ABC = rs, where r is the inradius and s is the semiperimeter. The semiperimeter s = (x + 10 + x + 8 + 18)/2 = x + 18. The area of ∆ABC = 90 = 4(x + 18) ⇒ x + 18 = 90/4 = 22.5 ⇒ x = 4.5. Therefore, AB = 4.5 + 10 = 14.5 cm and AC = 4.5 + 8 = 12.5 cm.
